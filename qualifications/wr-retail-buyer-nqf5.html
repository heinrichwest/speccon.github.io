Qualification Overview

The Occupational Certificate: Retail Buyer (NQF Level 5) provides comprehensive knowledge and skills for strategic procurement roles in retail environments. This qualification is designed for individuals seeking specialist positions in retail buying, vendor management, and merchandise procurement.

Students will gain strategic skills in product sourcing, supplier selection and negotiation, merchandise buying, stock allocation, and supplier relationship management while developing analytical competencies for retail procurement roles.

Learning Modules

Module 1: Sourcing Products and Selecting Suppliers (36 Credits)

  • • Concepts and principles for identifying and sourcing products and selecting suppliers (7 Credits)
  • • Source products and identify preferred suppliers (9 Credits)
  • • Processes and procedures for identifying preferred suppliers (8 Credits)
  • • Processes and procedures for sourcing products and setting ranges (12 Credits)

Module 2: Buying Merchandise (42 Credits)

  • • Concepts and principles of buying merchandise (6 Credits)
  • • Negotiate with suppliers (4 Credits)
  • • Buy products (7 Credits)
  • • Processes and procedures for negotiating, buying and recording products purchased (25 Credits)

Module 3: Allocating Stock to Stores (21 Credits)

  • • Concepts and principles for allocating stock to stores (3 Credits)
  • • Plan and allocate stock to stores (6 Credits)
  • • Processes and procedures for allocating stock to stores (12 Credits)

Module 4: Managing Stock and Supplier Relationships (32 Credits)

  • • Concepts and principles for managing supplier relationships and the performance of merchandise (10 Credits)
  • • Manage supplier and stock performance (10 Credits)
  • • Manage stock through the supply chain (4 Credits)
  • • Processes and procedures for the management of supplier and stock performance (8 Credits)
