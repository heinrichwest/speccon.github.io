QUALIFICATION OVERVIEW
======================

This qualification is designed to enable junior personnel to progress towards farm labour positions in mixed farming systems. The qualification provides learners with the opportunity to participate in agricultural processes for both plant and animal production while performing directed activities and guiding others in a mixed farming context.

Key objectives of this qualification include:
• Participating in agricultural processes combining plant and animal production under supervision
• Performing directed activities and providing guidance to other team members
• Gaining competency in multiple agricultural sub-fields including small stock, large stock, dairy, vegetable production, and hydroponic systems
• Making responsible decisions within familiar contexts based on agri-business principles
• Understanding and implementing agricultural practices and technical skills in mixed farming environments
• Developing fundamental competencies in communication, mathematics, and data management
• Learning agri-business principles including enterprise selection, production management, and marketing
• Implementing good agricultural practices including food safety, quality management, and environmental conservation
• Acquiring practical skills in both animal production (feeding, breeding, health) and plant production (cultivation, harvesting, pest management)

This qualification prepares learners for progression to higher management and technical production roles and allows horizontal movement between different agricultural sub-fields. It also supports recognition of prior learning and accommodates learners with varying backgrounds and experience levels.

COURSE CONTENT
==============

Module 1: Mathematical Literacy
- Apply basic knowledge of statistics and probability to influence the use of data and procedures in order to investigate life related problems
- Demonstrate understanding of rational and irrational numbers and number system
- Identify, describe, compare, classify, and explore shape and motion in 2-and 3-dimensional shapes in different contexts
- Use mathematics to investigate and monitor the financial aspects of personal and community life
- Work with a range of patterns and functions and solve problems

Module 2: Communication
- Accommodate audience and context needs in oral communication
- Interpret and use information from texts
- Use language and communication in occupational learning programmes
- Write texts for a range of communicative contexts

Module 3: Macadamia Production
- Control pest and weeds in tea fields
- Fertilize macadamia orchard
- Hole plant stations for planting macadamia trees
- Irrigate macadamia orchards
- Plant macadamia trees
- Prune macadamia trees
- Peg plant stations for macadamia orchards
- Scout macadamia orchard
- Train or selectively prune macadamia trees

Module 4: Macadamia Harvesting
- Harvest macadamia nuts in husk
- Dehusk the macadamia nut in the husk
- Take in nuts in the husk
- Dispatch kernel

Module 5: Electives
- Perform basic life support and/or first aid procedures in emergencies
- Operate in a team
- Plan and manage time in the workplace
- Maintaining occupational health, safety and general housekeeping

CAREER OPPORTUNITIES
====================

Upon successful completion of this qualification, learners can pursue the following career paths:

• Farm Labourer/Operator in mixed farming systems
• Agricultural Worker in various specializations:
  - Small stock production
  - Large stock production
  - Dairy production
  - Vegetable production
  - Hydroponic production
  - Organic farming
  - Combined plant and animal production
• Junior Farm Supervisor
• Team Leader in agricultural operations

This qualification also provides opportunities for:
• Progression to higher agricultural qualifications at NQF Level 3
• Advancement towards management and technical production practices
• Horizontal movement between different agricultural sub-fields
• Active participation in the primary agricultural sector
• Access to local, national, and international agricultural markets
• Career development in diverse mixed farming operations
• Recognition of prior learning and practical experience
